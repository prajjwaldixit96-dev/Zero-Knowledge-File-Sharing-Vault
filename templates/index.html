// PROTOCOL

How ZK Vault Works

A cryptographic guarantee that your data remains private, always.

01
Key Derivation

Your master password is hashed with PBKDF2-SHA256 to derive an encryption key. This key never leaves your device.

02
Client-Side Encryption

Files are encrypted in your browser using AES-256-GCM with a unique IV before any data is transmitted.

03
Secure Upload

Only ciphertext reaches our servers. We store encrypted blobs with zero ability to decrypt or read your files.

04
On-Demand Decryption

When you download, decryption happens locally in your browser. Your key, your device, your files.

zkvault_encrypt.py — zero_knowledge_protocol
$ zkvault encrypt --file secret_doc.pdf → Deriving key from master password (PBKDF2-SHA256, 100k iters)... → Generating unique IV: 8f3a2d1e9c4b7a06 → Encrypting with AES-256-GCM... ✓ Encrypted: secret_doc.pdf [2.4MB → 2.4MB ciphertext]   $ zkvault upload --encrypted secret_doc.pdf.enc → Uploading ciphertext to ZK Vault servers... → Key: [NEVER TRANSMITTED] ✓ Upload complete.
